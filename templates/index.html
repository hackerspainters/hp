Hackers and Painters?

hackersandpainters.sg is a lunch-time skills sharing event for UI/UX designers and software/hardware hackers to interact and share their skills and know-how in an informal setting. We welcome domain experts as well - for example, a lawyer sharing legal know-how or a trader sharing specific skills relating to stock market trading --- no "get-rich-quick" schemes though :-p.

The emphasis is on skills/knowledge sharing over a short 30 minutes to 1 hour session and polished presentations are *not* expected. This event is currently held in Singapore but we do want to expand the ethos and culture of knowledge sharing beyond Singapore.

In fact, in a completely distributed manner if possible.

Origins

Capitalizing on the constraints of lunch hours to learn new things! - http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brown_bag_seminar

A brown bag seminar, session or lunch is generally a training or information session during a lunch break. "Brown bag" is representative of meals brought along by the attendees, or provided by the host. In the USA, those are often packed in brown paper bags. Brown bag seminars normally run an hour or two.

The name of this event is inspired from http://www.paulgraham.com/hp.html

When I finished grad school in computer science I went to art school to study painting. A lot of people seemed surprised that someone interested in computers would also be interested in painting. They seemed to think that hacking and painting were very different kinds of work-- that hacking was cold, precise, and methodical, and that painting was the frenzied expression of some primal urge.

Both of these images are wrong. Hacking and painting have a lot in common. In fact, of all the different types of people I've known, hackers and painters are among the most alike.

What hackers and painters have in common is that they're both makers. Along with composers, architects, and writers, what hackers and painters are trying to do is make good things. They're not doing research per se, though if in the course of trying to make good things they discover some new technique, so much the better.

Where and when?

To find out more about the topic and the speaker(s) for specific Fridays, head over to our facebook group's event listing here -- https://www.facebook.com/groups/hackerspainters/events/ and here -- https://www.facebook.com/groups/generalhackers/events/.

We currently have two facebook groups because we have just renamed our event name from "General Hackers" to "Hackers and Painters", which is more reflective of the diversity of our speakers' background. The "General Hackers" group will be deprecated in a month's time when our existing community members move over and our currently scheduled sessions are also moved over to the "Hackers and Painters" group.
